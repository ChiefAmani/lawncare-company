# CAMPAIGN_STRATEGY.md

## Product/Service Overview and Unique Value Proposition
This campaign targets homeowners seeking professional, reliable, and high-quality lawncare services. Our unique value proposition is providing hassle-free, aesthetically pleasing lawn maintenance, allowing customers to enjoy their outdoor spaces without the time and effort commitment. Services include mowing, trimming, edging, fertilization, weed control, and seasonal clean-ups.

## Target Audience Personas

### Persona 1: Busy Professional Homeowner
*   **Name:** Sarah Chen
*   **Age:** 38
*   **Job:** Marketing Manager
*   **Pain Points:** Lacks time for yard work due to demanding career and family commitments. Values convenience and a well-maintained home exterior for curb appeal.
*   **Online Behavior:** Uses Facebook and Instagram for local business recommendations, searches Google for "lawn care near me," reads online reviews.

### Persona 2: Active Senior
*   **Name:** Robert Davis
*   **Age:** 67
*   **Job:** Retired Engineer
*   **Pain Points:** Physically unable or finds it difficult to perform strenuous lawn maintenance. Desires a neat and tidy yard without the physical strain.
*   **Online Behavior:** Uses Facebook to connect with community groups, relies on recommendations from friends and family, may use Google for service searches.

## Competitor Analysis
Competitors typically include local independent lawn care businesses and larger franchise operations. Their content often focuses on before/after photos, service lists, and seasonal tips. Many utilize Facebook for community engagement and Instagram for visual showcases. Common content includes promotions, customer testimonials, and basic service explanations. Differentiation often comes from pricing, service bundles, and perceived reliability/customer service.

## Platform Strategy

### Facebook
*   **Rationale:** Strong for local targeting (homeowners 35-65), community building, and sharing in-depth content.
*   **Best Practices:** Regular updates, photo albums of completed work, engaging with comments, running local targeted ads, sharing seasonal tips, promoting special offers. Utilize Facebook Reels for short, engaging videos.

### Instagram
*   **Rationale:** Visually driven platform, excellent for showcasing high-quality work and team personality.
*   **Best Practices:** High-quality before/after photos, short video clips (Reels, Stories) of work in progress, team introductions, behind-the-scenes content. Use Stories for quick updates and polls.

### YouTube (Optional)
*   **Rationale:** For longer-form content like DIY tips, seasonal guides, or detailed service explanations, building authority.
*   **Best Practices:** Short tutorials (e.g., "How to identify common weeds"), seasonal lawn care guides, customer testimonials.

## Messaging Pillars
1.  **Convenience & Time-Saving:** "Reclaim your weekends. Let us handle the hard work."
2.  **Beautiful & Healthy Lawns:** "Achieve the lush, green lawn you've always dreamed of."
3.  **Professional Expertise:** "Experienced and reliable care for a picture-perfect yard."
4.  **Local & Trusted Service:** "Your neighborhood lawn care experts."

## Content Types Per Platform

### Facebook
*   **Text Posts:** Seasonal tips, service explanations, customer testimonials.
*   **Image Posts:** Before/after photos, team photos, infographics.
*   **Carousels:** Step-by-step guides (e.g., "5 Steps to a Greener Lawn").
*   **Stories/Reels:** Quick updates, behind-the-scenes, short promotions.

### Instagram
*   **Image Posts:** High-quality before/after photos, close-ups of healthy lawns.
*   **Reels:** Time-lapses of lawn transformations, quick tips, team highlights.
*   **Stories:** Polls (e.g., "What's your biggest lawn challenge?"), Q&A, quick promotions.

## Posting Cadence
*   **Facebook:** 3-4 times per week.
*   **Instagram:** 4-5 times per week (mix of posts, Reels, Stories).
*   **YouTube (if used):** 1-2 times per month.

## Tone of Voice Guidelines
*   **Adjectives:** Friendly, Professional, Knowledgeable, Reliable, Approachable.
*   **Do:** Use clear, concise language. Be helpful and informative. Maintain a positive and enthusiastic tone.
*   **Don't:** Use overly technical jargon without explanation. Be overly aggressive in sales. Sound impersonal or robotic.
