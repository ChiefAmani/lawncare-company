# FINANCIAL_MODEL_DETAILED.md

## 1. Executive Summary
This detailed financial model provides a 12-month projection with three scenarios (Bear, Base, Bull) for revenue, costs, and cash flow, incorporating service expansion and risk factors.

## 2. Key Assumptions (Base Case)

### Revenue Drivers
*   **Average Client Value (ARPU):** $200/month (Mowing+ bundle, 12-month level billing).
*   **Client Acquisition Rate (New Clients/Month):**
    *   Month 1-3: 30
    *   Month 4-6: 40
    *   Month 7-9: 50
    *   Month 10-12: 60 (due to service expansion upsells)
*   **Churn Rate:** 3% monthly.
*   **Upsell Rate (Fertilization/Cleanups):** 10% of existing clients per month, generating an additional $50/month per upsold client (average).

### Cost Structure
*   **Variable Costs per Client (COGS):**
    *   Fuel: $10/client/month
    *   Supplies (blades, string, minor repairs): $5/client/month
    *   Direct Labor: $50/client/month
    *   Upsell Service Variable Cost: $15/upsell client/month (lower labor/material for specialized services)
    *   **Total Variable Cost (Mowing):** $65/client/month
*   **Fixed Costs (Monthly):**
    *   Owner Salary: $5,000 (Month 1-6), $7,000 (Month 7-12)
    *   Marketing/Advertising: $1,000 (Month 1-6), $1,500 (Month 7-12, for LSA expansion)
    *   Software (CRM, scheduling): $150
    *   Insurance: $200
    *   Equipment Maintenance/Lease: $1,000 (Month 1-6), $1,500 (Month 7-12, for additional crew equipment)
    *   Office/Admin: $300
    *   Part-time Helper (Month 3-6): $2,000/month
    *   Full-time Crew Lead (Month 7-12): $4,000/month
    *   **Total Fixed Costs (Month 1-2):** $7,650
    *   **Total Fixed Costs (Month 3-6):** $9,650
    *   **Total Fixed Costs (Month 7-12):** $14,350

## 7. Top 3 Risks (Severity x Probability)

1.  **Execution Risk (Scaling Labor & Operations):** (P:4, I:5) = 20
    *   *Description:* Difficulty hiring and retaining reliable 2-man crews, leading to service quality issues, missed appointments, and inability to scale. Route density optimization may be harder to achieve in practice.
    *   *Mitigation:* Implement robust hiring and training program. Offer competitive wages and benefits. Utilize scheduling software for optimal route planning.
2.  **Market Risk (Competitive Pricing Pressure):** (P:4, I:4) = 16
    *   *Description:* Local competitors may aggressively cut prices, forcing a reduction in ARPU or loss of clients, impacting gross margins.
    *   *Mitigation:* Emphasize premium service quality and customer experience. Differentiate through specialized services (e.g., organic treatments). Monitor competitor pricing closely.
3.  **Financial Risk (Underestimated Startup Costs/Cash Flow):** (P:3, I:4) = 12
    *   *Description:* Initial equipment purchases, unexpected repairs, or slower-than-expected client acquisition could deplete cash runway before profitability is achieved.
    *   *Mitigation:* Secure adequate initial funding. Maintain a cash reserve. Closely monitor actual vs. projected expenses and client acquisition. Delay non-essential capital expenditures.
